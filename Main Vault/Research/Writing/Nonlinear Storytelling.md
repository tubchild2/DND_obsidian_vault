### Branching Narratives
- Examples
	- Text based adventures
	- Choose your own adventure
	- Tabletop games
- Issues
	- Exponential growth
	- The medium in which you tell the story in can make this exponential growth issue greater
	- They prune the tree of options by
		- Giving you fake choices that collapse back into the same place
		- Making some stories end early, and others go on longer
- Advantages
	- Roleplaying
	- Immersion
	- Writers choose between player autonomy and fleshed out protagonists
	- Exploration

### Looping Narratives
- Examples
	- Groundhog Day
- Issues
	- Makes sense for people going through it the first and second time
- Advantages
	- Makes story feel more cohesive
	- Works well in games
	- Diegetic looping is when the loop is part of the plot
	- Allows you to examine the same story from multiple different angles
	- Different characters in the same situations give you a broader understanding

### Meta-Narratives
- Example
	- Narrative of discovering a story
- Explanation
	- Games create stories
		- Emergent experiences shape the narrative the players engage with
		- You don't always know what that story might look like
	- Stories create games
		- Finding meaning in art is more important than the meaning itself
		- Actively consuming media can be seen as a game in that sense
		- You don't know what players might do, or if they're playing at all
	- Fabula and Syuzhet
		- Fabula is the chronical sequence of events
		- Syuzhet is the sequence of events as they're presented to the player
	- Video games are uniquely synthesized for both of these
	- When the Syuzhet is so scrambled, the player discovering the story is a game?
	- You know how the players will interact with the story because of this scrambling and discovery
	- Underlying narrative that's split into chunks --> a game of assembling those chunks --> a narrative over top of that of how you assembled those chunks.

### Combinatorial Narrative
- The writer writes a bunch of chunks of story
- Random pool of events that combine into a new narrative
- Write generically to allow it to reorder into something new
- Creates a much wider range of stories
- Amount of stories you can tell grows combinatorically with the amount of events you write
- This happens in TTRPGs
- Players choose the order of events to shape the game how they'd like
