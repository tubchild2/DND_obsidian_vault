#### The [[Serendipita|Ideal]] of Orphans
Appears sporadically throughout [[Serendipita]], requests an amount of money from a selected individual, and then (if not paid) unleashes an unavoidable 6d6 Psychic damage attack and vanishes. 


#### [[AOTH]] - Chaos
Represents the fundamental concept of chaos and discord. Possesses functionally limitless power and cannot die in the traditional sense. He can warp reality and psychically overpower anything. 

Typically, [[AOTH]] resides in his domain at the edge of the universe, where he creates and manages chaos throughout the universe subconsciously. However, occasionally, he can be attracted to unnatural amounts of order and harmony. He is vulnerable to it, but he seeks to undo it subconsciously. He behaves entirely off instinct and has no real consciousness. 

Only objects of unbelievable power can potentially distract or and send him away, but otherwise, he cannot be stopped. The only known object in [[Serendipita]] potentially capable of doing that is [[AOTH's Pendant]]. 

As he approaches, his presence is felt and known with progressively greater understanding. Some beings may appear to be mentally overwhelmed and obsessed with him and his coming presence. The impending doom causes a wide variety of indulgent and panicked behaviors. 

Everything fears [[AOTH]]. Nothing can stop [[AOTH]]. Impermanence and chaos is one of the most ancient concepts in existence, and it cannot be erased. 


#### Small Goodman - Lawyers
The only cosmic halfling lawyer you'll ever need. "Better Call Small!"

A sleazy, manipulative, thrill-seeking, greedy lawyer. He's excellent at what he does, but his methods are questionable at best. He's charismatic, garish, and prone to mistakes, but he's never lost a case. 

His office is currently located in the Timeless Bazaar. His phone is accessible to anyone. Most of his work revolves around pro-bono cases to settle disputes revolving around the Player's Handbook. 

He is shockingly revered. He's capable of doing a lot of things by convincing people that they're supposed to happen. 

He's close with Debt. 


#### Madame Mora - Debt
Appears to settle cosmic debts. If someone made a deal with an [[Serendipita|ideal]] for something really bad and didn't return the favor, she appears to settle it. If it can't be settled, the person dies. 

A bureaucrat of the highest order. Hates her job, but views it as incredibly important to the nature of the universe. Like most creatures, doesn't enjoy inflicting pain. Instead, she tries to work with people to pay off their debts before it's too late. 

Works with Small Goodman on occasion, and is the punishment of a lot of cosmic crimes. 


#### Verda - Life
A gentle gardener who resides in a peaceful paradise planet. Wants life to last forever, and makes it so. Protects whatever living plants/creatures it can, and finds death unnecessary and unnatural. Life was meant to go on and be in balance with itself, not end prematurely. People can do so much more!


#### Carter Hayes - Survival
The Survivor was originally a man named Carter Hayes. He was incredibly unlucky, and (due to a horrible turn of events) found himself at odds with the [[Serendipita|Ideal]] of Debt. After pulling some strings, he was rescued by the [[Serendipita|Ideal]] of Life, and merged with the [[Serendipita|ideal]] of survival. 

Unfortunately, this meant he would be forced to be present at and experience every large-scale tragedy throughout the cosmos. He cannot die, and is in a state of perfect health at all times. 

He is incredibly depressed due to constantly watching and evading every horrible thing that has happened in the past several centuries, being incapable of staying in one place for very long, and due to being completely and utterly alone. He enjoys adventuring, and he enjoys travel. He tries to help people whenever he can, and is a genuinely heroic individual. 

He's in a tricky situation with the other Ideals. Verda and Time of course love him, but Madame Mora, Death, and Small Goodman don't. He's violating the typical rules of life and existing long past his time to fill a responsibility he didn't earn nor is he qualified for. 


#### The Twins - Time and Death
Nihilistic twins. They encourage people to give up on trying to make things better and to instead savor the time they have left. They believe in the natural order of things. 

Death doesn't just want to kill people; he wants people to die when they're ready. Death as a concept isn't something he thinks should be viewed as an unstoppable killing machine, he views it as something with more nuance than that. 

Time cares about permanence and change. He knows most things don't last, so he doesn't see them as all that important. He isn't against life, but he doesn't understand its value in the grand scheme of things. He cares mostly about serving his own interests, the interests of other Ideals, and the interests of things that *will* last. He is akin to the Mysterious Stranger in that sense. 

They didn't take names. They both view them as unnecessary and frivolous. 


#### Scratch - Creativity
A muse of the highest order. Loves to create things, be it art, music, stories, sculptures, food, whatever. Inspires people it deems as worthy once every few years. Appears as a tangled mess of scribbled lines. 

Occasionally makes suits for Small Goodman, though they have a back and forth relationship. Goodman has tried to end some inspired artists careers early on technicalities, which meant Scratch has to deal with Madame Mora behind the scenes to pay off the debts, which is an incredibly involved and irritating process.
