
### Mission Overview
##### Plot
- Introduction
	- The SMS-66 was detected drifting aimlessly in the outer rim 2 days ago. We have no record of it. It appears to be a Class 4 Cargo Ship. 
	- You've been paid to board and reactivate the ship. You then need to pilot it back to a salvaging bay if possible. If impossible, report your findings after a thorough inspection, and we'll decide if we should send a salvage crew. 
	- Every player has a communicator that allows them to speak with the captain. 
	- Good luck.
- Tasks
	- Scout
		- Abilities

			- You have scanning equipment for checking rooms for salvage purposes.

		- Briefing

		- Abilities

			- Fighter esque.
		- Briefing
			- None
	- Captain
		- Abilities
			- High charisma leader.
			- Bard esque. 
			- Armed with a pistol.
			- Capable of piloting the ship.
			- Has communication equipment that allows communication with any of the other players, but they can't communicate with each other; all communications go through the captain. 
		- Briefing
			- Informed of time limit (limited air, 3.5hrs real-time)
				- Encourage crew to split up. 
				- It'll take too long if they don't. 
				- The longer they're here, the more air they use up.
				- That's more dangerous than staying together. 
			- Information about what tasks must be accomplished
				1. Activate main breaker and generator in the engine terminal (engineer-only)
				2. Assess cause of death in the med bay (medic-only)
				3. Flip breaker 1 in med bay (requires communication with the engineer. Much harder without engineer, but not impossible)
				4. Check for survivors in the crew quarters (flooded, soldier-only)
				5. Flip breaker 2 in crew quarters (requires communication with the engineer. Much harder without engineer, but not impossible)
				6. Once ship is powered, pilot ship to SMS Salvage 28 (captain-only. Much harder without the captain, but not impossible)
				7. If recovery is impossible, once the ship is powered, return to your ship, open the bay doors, and leave to report your findings.

- Brain worm that can only infect people if they're isolated because it emits a bunch of really bright flashing colorful lights. Infected players work against the party to isolate and infect everyone else. The only thing that changes is their intentions and eye color (mention each players eye color in the very beginning and never again to give infected plausible deniability). Make sure players know beforehand that infections occur silently and they have to go along with them. One player is infected near the beginning to begin the conflict. If the party completes their task on the planet/ship, they win. If everyone is infected, they lose. Works best if players have multiple tasks throughout the map and/or separate skillsets that require separation. Make sure infection conditions are vague enough for deniability but strong enough so that people can argue. The goal is paranoia, distrust, and lack of information.
