A sequel to [[Arcohaem]]

After the events of [[Arcohaem]], a wealth of order and harmony emerged in [[Arcohaem]]. This, along with the work of some devotees using [[AOTH's Pendant]] (which was found in Malfaust's Lair), has attracted [[AOTH]] to [[Arcohaem]]. He begins moving from the edge of the universe towards [[Serendipita]]. He'll be there in a few weeks.

Set in a smaller town. They occasionally leave via portals and other forms of transportation, but they usually return to the town to interact with and observe the other characters.

##### ACT 1
As he draws closer, the residents of [[Arcohaem]] experience several side effects
- Everyone realizes that he's coming and that they're going to die. Majora's Mask esque events play out as they try to either escape or enjoy the rest of their lives. Nightmares become increasingly intense and the image of [[AOTH]] grows clearer and clearer. 
- [[Arcohaem]] becomes trapped and no one can leave. No matter what they try, something prevents them from leaving. A powerful storm eventually engulfs the region, preventing any entry in or out. 
- Some begin experiencing symptoms of Chaos. They become obsessed with [[AOTH]] and succumb to their natural chaotic desires.  
- [[AOTH's Pendant]] is the big ticket item of this act. If they can get their hands on that, they could potentially undo this all. They see how just an item capable of using a fraction of [[AOTH]]'s power is already enough to bring [[Arcohaem]] to its knees and completely destroy its user. Let them fill in the gaps in their own heads about what nightmarish entity they think [[AOTH]] is. They can only really get it by doing some quests around the city, letting them get to know the townsfolk even better. They *barely* get the pendant off the user. He was a victim of it and is oblivious to what is going on due to being completely corrupted by [[AOTH]]'s power.

##### ACT 2
The heroes must attempt to stop its arrival using [[AOTH's Pendant]]. It allows them to travel to and meet other ideals across the cosmos. 

Things get artsy and existential as they leave the bounds of [[Serendipita]] and interact with the cosmic elements of the universe. This second segment takes inspiration from Adventure Time. 

Throughout the story, the players watch people become hopeless, and indulgent. They see the odds stacked further and further against them. They're pressured over and over to give in themselves, because why would it matter anyways? Their interactions with people become much worse and bleaker as things go on. 

They see
- The beauty and peace of the cosmos, and of life and love via the [[Serendipita|Ideal]] of Life. A gardener and provider. She tells them that they have a responsibility to help those people. 
- The absurdity, humor, and ultimate strangeness of the life everyone leads.
- The warmness that's possible no matter what the circumstances.
- Hopelessness via the [[Serendipita|Ideal]] brothers time and sin. They show them cataclysms, good people who got nothing in return and died, and people who seemed morally impregnable who became villains. No one is immune and morality can fluctuate depending on the time you live in. They tell them to quit trying to stop [[AOTH]] and enjoy their lives while they still can. They're both nihilists, but they aren't mean. They want what is best for the party, but what they believe is difficult to swallow. 
- Relationships begin to crumble around them, and their lives get much worse in light of [[AOTH]]'s arrival. In perspective, it all seems so small. 
- Nothing they try has any effect and it just seems worse and worse. They're pressured to quit and indulge and let [[Arcohaem]] die. 
- An antagonist like the mysterious stranger. Doesn't care about humans at all. They could die and be replaced by him incredibly quickly, and the things they do are meaningless in comparison to his power. 
- Their party member pushed further away from them all and their relationship strained even more.

##### ACT 3
They return to [[Arcohaem]] as [[AOTH]] arrives. His arrival summons horrific monstrosities and distorts reality as he draws closer. Wither Storm / Weirdmaggeddon inspiration. He is truly unstoppable.

The climax involves them attempting to banish [[AOTH]] (not kill) from [[Arcohaem]] temporarily. But, as it approaches, reality begins to distort, and it begins tempting and pushing them as hard as it can. It shows them dead lovers, their worst nightmares, and the horrific history of the culture they're *protecting*. 

The main drive of this climax will be [[AOTH]] using their own friends and family against them. It has limitless power and resources, so it will use anything it can to break them. It's going to push people that they love to their breaking points, and use that against the heroes. All the people they've come to know, and all the emotional buildup in their relationships will be used against them in really dramatic fashion. [[AOTH]] wants to break them emotionally, even subconsciously, and its role as an unstoppable force makes it all the more hopeless. At this point, pay off having one of the players turn on the party. Dark, dark moment. 

Humans are flawed, selfish, violent, weak, and transient. But, no matter how bad things may be, and no matter how meaningless morality may feel, you can always do what is right, and that has value even without reward. At their lowest point, they can choose humanity, order, and unity. They can resist giving in. This will stun [[AOTH]], and leave him susceptible to banishment. He'll go away, but the people of [[Arcohaem]] will have suffered irreversible emotional harm and are inarguably worse off. 

The story ends there, on kind of a bleak note. Yes, they saved [[Arcohaem]], but they lost things that were much more meaningful and had their emotions toyed with and broken by an eldritch being that didn't even know they were there and was acting entirely on base subconscious instinct. They lost friends, family, relationships, belongings, meaning, and anything else it could tear from them. They gained hope, though, and a persistent understanding that life sucks, but doing the right thing anyway is powerful nonetheless.
