## **THESES**
Refined Thesis
- Absolute power corrupts absolutely, and corruption thrives in systems. 
	- Man is inherently prone to sin and will indulge when he can justify it
	- Perfect leadership is by definition impossible
	- The systems man constructs are harmful to their mental and moral wellbeing
	- Technological progress often means moral or mental degradation
	- Truth fades the more filters it goes through
- However, you always have a choice to do the right thing.
	- There are two moral compasses: the compass of the world, and the objective compass.
	- You can choose to do what is objectively correct, and not just what the world deems acceptable
	- Doing the wrong thing often means prosperity, and has much a greater impact
	- Doing the right thing often means loss, and doesn't have that grand of an impact
	- However good does make things better, even if only by a little.

Side Theses
- Republic vs [[Frigoshia]]
	- Republic
		- The Republic believes progress is important for humanity. 
		- They like new ideas, a new government, a new form of technology.
		- They're also incredibly susceptible to corruption by abandoning the human aspect of rulership.
	- [[Frigoshia]]
		- Industrial society replaces the human experience with artificial surrogate experiences.
		- Technology forces humans to adapt, not the other way around.
		- Technology will inevitably lead to greater suffering, catastrophe, genetic engineering abuses, and the loss of human control.
		- Modern individuals are over-socialized, meaning they're conditioned to suppress their natural instincts to conform to society's rules and function like components of a machine.
		- The industrial system can't be reformed. They advocate for its complete destruction to allow for a return to pre-industrial, "wild" ways of life.
	- *They're both wrong!*
		- The Republic is susceptible to corruption, is callous, and is ultimately leading to its own destruction.
		- By ignoring changes, [[Frigoshia]] leaves itself completely vulnerable, and prevents any growth.

## **PLOTS**
Primary Plots
	[[Malfaust]]'s Plot
		[[Frigoshia]] is breeding dragons!
		Uncovering the propaganda of the Republic
		Discovering hints of a conspiracy
		Uncovering the corruption of the Republic
		Finding out [[Terrik|the Wraith]] accelerated the war on both sides
		Finding out [[Terrik|the Wraith]] is responsible for the Republic weapons division and for the Dragon eggs
		[[Malfaust]] is going to seize control, use the weapons/dragons to conquer [[Serendipita]], and reclaim [[Serendipita]]
	Wolves' Story
		They start completely indoctrinated into the beliefs of the Republic
		They have their beliefs about [[Frigoshia]] and the Republic reinforced
		They meet an exception to their beliefs about [[Frigoshia|Frigoshians]]
		Their incredibly loyal overseer was likely framed but you can't prove it
		They nearly die due to the "incompetence" of the Chancellor
		Their beliefs about [[Frigoshia]] are greatly challenged
		They begin to uncover corruption, and they learn how much immorality pays
		They learn about [[Terrik]], and how he used to be like them
		They're presented with the choice between prosperity and morality
		They're presented with a choice between repeating the cycle of propaganda and racial hatred, or sparing a wyrmling that imprinted on them.

Side Plots
	[[Terrik]]'s Story
		He was a prominent soldier, and far and away the greatest and most praised in [[Arcohaem]].
		In an attempt to uproot Republic corruption, he accidentally took a life in self-defense.
		He slowly became corrupt while trying to cover up his work and uproot more corruption.
		In an ironic twist, in an attempt to uproot corruption, someone killed his fiancé.
		[[Terrik]] got his revenge, but was caught, and lost everything. He escaped his execution.
		He lost all hope in government, and then met [[Malfaust]].
		He agreed with [[Malfaust]], so became his hand, and learned from him.
		[[Terrik]] shows what happens to people in power, who little by little give in to corruption.
		He's beyond redemption, and he believes [[Malfaust]] is right.
	[[Kragknull]]'s Story
		He was the Wolves' overseer
		He discovered corruption in the Senate, and attempted catch someone in the act
		He broke some minor laws with [[Finn|Senator Finn]] to do it
		He was caught, framed for the murder he was attempting to solve, and exiled.
		He investigated [[Kaul]], and learned that he was meeting with [[Terrik|the Wraith]].
		He meets back up with the Wolves, and helps them steal proof
		He is killed by [[Terrik]] on a whim.

## **PLOT OVERVIEW**
Act 1
This act demonstrates that the wolves are fully indoctrinated into the belief system of the Republic regarding their own nation, and [[Frigoshia]].
- Part 1: Introduction to world and to their mission.
- Part 2: Make them hate [[Frigoshia|Frigoshians]]
- Part 3: Make them feel pride for the Republic

Act 2
Sowing seeds of doubt
- Part 4: Some [[Frigoshia|Frigoshians]] are just people / [[Terrik|the Wraith]] is horrific
- Part 5: The Republic is incredibly suspicious
- Part 6: [[Frigoshia]] is dying, but they don't show it. Citizens and government are two wholly different animals.  

Act 3
Learning the truth
- Part 7: [[Atka]] has been collaborating with [[Terrik|the Wraith]]. They are close to finishing "[[Amalgamma]]" using the dragon eggs "M" provided. An upcoming Gala has something to do with it.
- Part 8: Infiltration of a Gala with the help of [[Finn|Senator Finn]], who recognizes them and tells them what happened to [[Kragknull]]. [[Kaul]] has been funding the project (and "M's" weapons), he murdered the Republic banker, and he framed [[Kragknull]]. It was likely an interception of one of these shipments that caused the Wolves' mission. The shipments are moving through a redistribution center to prevent any documentation. They're caught and branded traitors.
- Part 9: [[Terrik|The Wraith]] is [[Terrik|Captain Terrik]]. [[Malfaust]] the Red is plotting to seize [[Arcohaem]] in a few weeks, and [[Terrik]] has been serving him. They learn where [[Malfaust]]'s lair is.

Act 4
Saving [[Arcohaem]]
- Part 10: Making a plan, returning to the Republic as "traitors," and witnessing the other side of the hate. Surviving an ambush from their old allies: [[the Karlstad Crusaders]]. 
- Part 11: Storming the lair, and learning what [[Amalgamma]] really is, and what the war's really been about. 
- Part 12: The final battle and moral test.
