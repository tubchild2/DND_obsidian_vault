Hardware
- Multiport repeaters
	- Converts digital signals into different digital signals
	- Regenerates signal for longer distance
- Media converters
	- Converts electric and optical signals
- Modem
	- Converts analog and digital signals
- Hub
	- Connects multiple computers together
- Network Interface Card (NIC)
	- Connects computer to medium (ethernet / wireless)
- Cables or electromagnetic waves in space
	- Coaxial
	- Twisted pair
	- Electromagnetic waves
	- Fiberoptic
- Connectors
- Network hardware that looks only at bits

Physical and Logical Circuits
Physical Circuits
- There's only one path
- Signals go from one place to another
- There's no variation
Logical Circuits
- The actual way the signals flow
- Regardless of physical connectivity
- Can also refer to multiple paths

Analog vs Digital
- Analog is real world / physical
- Digital is based on discrete steps. 0 and 1 cannot represent 0.3 for instance.
- You can't ever get a complete recreation of analog because analog can go to an infinite number of variations while digital eventually has to break down to 0s and 1s. 
- Digitizing loses some information, so the more samples you have, and the more bits you got, the closer you can get
- Analog computers don't use ones and zeroes?!
- Bounded/guided media are wires, unbounded/unguided is wireless

Circuit Configuration
- How data can travel from one computer to another
- Point to point
- Point to multipoint
- Simplex (one way)
- Half Duplex (two way, one at a time)
- Full Duplex (two way)
- Multiplexing (a whole lot of ways at once)
