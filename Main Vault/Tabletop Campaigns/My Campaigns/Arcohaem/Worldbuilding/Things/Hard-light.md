### Origins
The work of Malfaust. Weapons of this caliber are granted only to those who've sworn their allegiance to Malfaust himself. Malfaust found and hoarded incredibly pure shards of Eathyst, then through an unstable draining device, he channeled raw, unfiltered kinetic energy into an Eathyst mold, which projected a blade he called Hard-Light.

### Properties
Hard-light is incredibly light, durable, sharp, bright, and hot, capable of mangling shields after a few direct strikes. Each strike releases a small burst of energy that makes them feel like hundreds of pounds of force were behind them. Additionally, due to its unique properties, it can pierce/deflect ether attacks and defenses. Unfortunately, it's incredibly dangerous, unpredictable, volatile, and expensive. A direct blow to the core of the weapon could cause it to detonate. It emits a whistling and deep warbling sound, and can be ignited on command. 

### Combat
Due to its design, it favors an especially fast and aggressive style of combat that's only used by incredibly experienced warriors. It's light enough to swing rapidly, it can rapidly break defenses/ether, and it's disorienting up close. However, prolonged fights are dangerous because of its lack of defensive capabilities and volatility. It's not meant to tank rapid strikes, as they could damage the core. It's also not fit for stealth because of how loud and bright ignition is.

### Culture
It's only been used by Terrik, but it's incredibly dangerous in his hands. Local legends have surfaced around his anonymous assassin form. Some people think he's a devil, others a monster, but no one thinks he's human, because no human fights like Terrik. The whistling noise sounds like screaming from afar, leading to rumors about screaming from certain dark valleys around the time Terrik claims a victim.
