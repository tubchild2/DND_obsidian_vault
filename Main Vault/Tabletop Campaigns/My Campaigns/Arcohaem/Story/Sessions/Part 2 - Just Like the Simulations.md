### Chapter 1: [[Ammalor]]

##### Purpose:
- Reintroduce the world and characters
- Let the Wolves charter a ship to Freyrhage
- Mention the blockade
- Tease the Draconic Era with the mysterious dagger

##### Plot:
Introduction
	In front of you sits the great Republic city of [[Ammalor]]. It's known far and wide for its incredible artisans, trade, and craftsmanship. A few days ago, you embarked here to charter a ship to sail as far north as you could, to allow you to enter [[Frigoshia]] via the Wastes Pale. You see bustling ports, shops, and streets lined with the rich and powerful. What do you do?

Shops

	- Jackets
	- Masks

- Imported spices
- Exotic foods
- Imported alochol

	- Nautical and Frost themes
	- Fancy Lanterns

Ports
- Most people see their military outfit and look away and hide
- One shady guy isn't afraid
	- His name is Marrek Renn. 
	- He's an older, sketchy sailor.
	- He asks for 500gp because of the volcanic activity in the north wiping out ships. He's sailing to Freyrhage anyways to make some shipments that are on a need to know basis.
	- Freyrhage is the best place he can take them because of the blockade. 
- Blah blah blah, they find a ship
- This part isn't all that important

Sailing
- They have to take tiny ships to avoid "volcanic activity" and to go undetected
- While on the ship, they have some quiet roleplay moments.
- Some mysterious noises come from the waters that the captain claims is just "volcanic activity," but they definitely sound organic. He's hiding something.
- Looking around the ship, they find a hidden box containing a rusted dagger with draconic emblems etched into it. The captain catches them, but isn't worried. He talks about it
	- It's old; impossibly old. The tribes of [[Arcohaem]] were founded in like ~122, but this is much, much older.
	- The craftsmanship is remarkably advanced for that period. So, if it's impossibly old, and impossibly advanced, then who made it?
	- He doesn't know, but it keeps him motivated to keep sailing because it reminds him that there's always more to discover about [[Arcohaem]] the deeper you look.


### Chapter 2: Borderlands

##### Purpose:
- Establish positive relationship with [[Kragknull|Commander Kragknull]] and Senator Finn
- Establish [[Frigoshia|Frigoshian]] presence in town of Freyrhage
- Establish stakes of the impending [[Frigoshia|Frigoshian]] raid
- Establish bond between the Wolves and the citizens
- Give the Wolves the mission of escorting people to the docks

##### Plot:
Freyrhage
- They dock at a port along the eastern cliffs of Freyrhage. They need to climb a steep winding road to reach the rest of the town. 
- Most people in this village hate the war. Their position on the front made them a frequent target. Freyrhage only agreed to the declaration of independence out of fear. After some of the things they've seen [[Frigoshia]] do, they decided they couldn't be a part of it anymore, no matter what it would cost them. They declared neutrality, and the military is approaching to wipe out the village. 
- The Persson family is incredibly sweet, but distressed. Grandma Persson can't walk, and won't be able to flee. Their large hairy Yak-like livestock snuggle up to the Wolves. They ask what the Wolves' mission is.
- The people are distressed. Some are packing and trying to flee, and others are indulging because they think they're going to die. Children are oblivious, and parents are horrified. People can't flee because the ports are charging too great of a price, and can only escort 5 additional people per boat.
- The invasion is tonight, and not everyone is going to make it out. No one can afford to!

[[Kragknull]]
- Small Talk
	- He wanted to test the communicator
	- Senator Finn pipes up in the background
	- They're talking about the dragon rumors that are spreading right now. Everyone and their mother thinks they've seen a dragon.
- Their Mission
	- He asks how they're doing
	- Freyrhage isn't associated with the Republic, and it's infinitely less important than their overall mission. There's also no way they can save everyone because they're hopelessly outnumbered.
	- However, they shouldn't abandon them all to die. 
	- They should escort as many people as they can to the docks and pay their fares.
	- It's a trolley problem situation, since no matter what, someone is going to die.
- Goodbye

	- He tells them to stay vigilant and focused



### Chapter 3: Termina

##### Purpose:
- Demonstrate the monstrousness of the [[Frigoshia|Frigoshians]]; make the players hate them
- Put players in a high-octane battle to get as many civilians out as possible as the battalion chases after them
- Destroy hope for a complete victory by destroying one of the ships
- Have an NPC sacrifice themselves for their mission, and for their family
- PRIMARY GOAL FOR SESSION: make a player cry

##### Plot:
Invasion
- Regardless of what they choose, the invasion begins
- The [[Frigoshia|Frigoshians]] are incredibly fast, violent, and animalistic
- They try to kill Grandma Persson
- They stab the Yak-like animals with their spears, and they take multiple hits to do so
- They're physically intimidating and hide their faces
- They burn down houses
- They pillage supplies for themselves
- They kill civilians
- Blood curdling screams echo through the valley
- After all this, they should want to help at least some people

Caravan
- In the chaos, they begin grabbing people to escort them to the docks and giving them money
- They form a caravan of wagons and begin riding out of the town
- The caravan is attacked. High octane combat breaks out, and citizens are shot, stabbed, and pulled from caravans left and right.
- They careen down the mountain pass and reach the docks
- The [[Frigoshia|Frigoshians]] begin shooting flaming arrows at the ships to try to burn them down, which complicates things. The people on the ships begin preparing to leave early, and are shooting muskets at the [[Frigoshia|Frigoshians]].
- One of the ships goes down, meaning not everyone they grabbed is going to make it out. They have to make snap decisions about who's getting out and who isn't.

Failure
- Play "A Tragedy" playlist
- They begin to hear thunderous footsteps in the distance. A [[Frigoshia|Frigoshian]] war beast is approaching: Remorhaz.
- The Persson father grabs them and tells them that it's too late. Their mission is more important than saving this town. He'll forever be grateful for what they've done. He hugs them. If they stay now, they're going to die. They need to run now! 
- As the Wolves run, he says goodbye to his children, and tells them that he's going to be ok, but that they need to get on the ship now.
- People pack onto the ships as they sail away. They're going to be ok.
- Captain Alric of the [[Frigoshia|Frigoshian]] military arrives riding the Remorhaz. He says that the tribe will pay for their stupidity and indifference. It is the responsibility of every Arcohaemian to defend their ancestry. He's deeply sorry, but it must be done to ensure that their culture isn't wiped out by Republic genocide.
- The beast tears through the survivors, including the Persson parents.
- The Wolves make it out.
