
### OVERALL PLOT
[[Knell]] from [[Koth]] has begun a campaign against the Kingdoms of [[Limina]]. He has an overwhelming advantage and has conquered half the realm. He wants to break the seals on the Pit of [[Tollwether]] so that he can bring his daughter back to life, but in doing so, he releases an eldritch horror that dominates the world for 500 years. The party has been sent from the future to stop him, but the eldritch horror has projected a sentinel back in time to make things harder for them. 


### CHAPTER BY CHAPTER (OLD)
ACT 1
Chapter 1
- It's 2002, in the land of [[Limina]]. They're young, like 14-16 years old each, and are young, optimistic, and hopeful. They're all kids, so they're flawed:
	- Impulsivity / Immaturity / Irresponsibility / Childishness
	- Insecurity / Shame / Loneliness / Awkwardness
	- Overconfidence / Pride
	- Selfishness / Greed
	- Cowardice / Fear / Anxiety
	- Impatience / Wrath
	- Hopelessness / Depression / Existential Dread
- 533 years ago, [[Knell]] kidnapped people of royal descent and used them to break the seal of the Pit, releasing an eldritch horror that has dominated the world.
- The survivors have found an ancient [[Tollwether]] gate that will allow them to travel back to 1469. They're going to either:
	- Kill [[Knell]]
	- Protect a descendant
	- Boost the seal
	- End a bloodline
- The party is a group of friends living in an underground village. They've been safe there for decades.
- The eldritch horror finds them, and sends its Hunters after them. It enters the camp, takes out the lights, and picks off the adults. The party must use the gate to escape, but they accidentally bring a Hunter with them.
- They're know in 1469, and with help from a mysterious benefactor, they have the quest to stop [[Knell]]. They optimistically make a plan, without fully grasping the scale of their responsibility. 

Chapter 2
- They learn who [[Knell]] was. He's described as a perfect human; he's fit, methodical, efficient, and incredibly intelligent. He follows a strict code of honor and has never lost a fight or war in his life. As they can tell from how things played out, he didn't lose this upcoming fight either.
- Their plan is far too optimistic; they get whooped. Not only do they lose, but [[Knell]] absolutely traumatizes them. He rubs his victory in, lets them run only for him to chase them, exploits all of their weaknesses, shatters them mentally, and destroys everything that matters to them. Additionally, he explains that if what they're saying really is true, then they have three issues:
	- Time likes to fight back, and there's a chance what they're doing now already happened, meaning it's canonical that they lose.
	- They're just kids, and they have no chance at beating a full army.
	- Even if they do win, their actions will erase their timeline from existence. That'll kill all of their friends and family, and possibly erase them from existence. That type of paradox could cause problems far worse than anything [[Knell]] ever does.
- He spares them, and leaves them stranded, only for them to be attacked by the Hunter, who they also can't really fight.
- After barely surviving, the real weight of the situation sinks in. It's entirely hopeless, they're no where near powerful enough to stop [[Knell]], and everyone is going to die to the horror. Things are even worse now that there's a piece of the horror here.
- They're pushed to their lowest lows, and forced to sit with their responsibilities, and with their regrets. If they want to stop [[Knell]], they need to grow up.
- They are met by the Mysterious Benefactor, who gives them advice. They form a new plan, in which (in the next month) they need to travel across [[Limina]] and secure the help of every royal family to defend [[Tollwether]]. 

Act 2
Chapters 3-9

	- They travel to a location, learn some lore, have an emotional moment in which they deal with one of the following issues, fight a boss, and then progress in the story.
	- They progress as characters from boys to men. They become literally stronger too.
	- They learn about [[Knell]], [[Tollwether]], the Horror, and the Primordials.
	- Chapters will typically focus on the local characters in a region. They need to convince them that they're from the future, and that [[Knell]] wants to open an ancient seal beneath [[Tollwether]], and that they need military support in under a month. These missions typically require some side questing.
	- If they fail, they'll learn the lesson the hard way. They can absolutely lose.

Act 3
Chapter 10
- They are rapidly approaching their rematch with [[Knell]]
- They're completely different people, and are significantly more powerful now
- The armies of [[Knell]] have been halted by the help they were able to secure, but [[Knell]] found a way: he met the Hunter and made a deal. The Hunter and him merged, allowing him the power to open the Seal and get past the armies. He's alone, but he's in the caverns rapidly approaching the seal. The party pursue him alone.
- Before the seal opens, [[Knell]], and the party are brought into a realm beyond time and space to prove themselves up for what must happen. It's the calm before the storm, and it's really tonally dissonant from everything else. The "people" here ask the party if they're truly ready to stop [[Knell]], if they've grown up, and if they understand what they must lose to move on. The "people" remind them of the lessons they've learned, and tell them to challenge [[Knell]] to a duel, as he's honor bound to it. The "people" are the ancients of [[Tollwether]], and they all look like the mysterious benefactor from the beginning. The seal is opening; it's time to face your destiny.
- The final battle is climactic, and everyone gets a chance to 1v1 [[Knell]]. Though a better warrior than any of them will ever be, he's not actually perfect; he's prideful, he lacks restraint, he's bound by honor, he's callous, he's selfish, he's insecure, and he is incapable of accepting his daughter's death. The party can hesitate, they can forgive themselves, and they can choose restraint. These mistakes will be his downfall.
- After everyone has their moment, they defeat [[Knell]] and the Horror, and close the seal. [[Knell]] asks them why they'd sacrifice everything they have for people they don't even know. He'd never understand. He's banished in the realm forever, and when they exit, he's gone.
- The party is taken by the ancients of [[Tollwether]] to live out the rest of their lives outside of time, as the paradox they created cannot be allowed to exist within this universe. This is functionally death, and the biggest sacrifice they can possibly make. They're forgotten forever.
- The people they helped live out the rest of their lives happily, the Horror remains trapped, and [[Knell]] is frozen out of time for the rest of eternity.
