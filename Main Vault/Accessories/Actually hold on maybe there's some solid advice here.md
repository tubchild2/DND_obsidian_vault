If you're interested in someone, make that clear and just say so and ask if they're interested also. It doesn't need to be (and in fact, shouldn't be) a grand romantic thing because then you're just setting yourself up for greater disappointment. Just be direct and don't make a big deal out of it. 

If you aren't in a place to have feelings for someone, or if you don't want to act on them, move the hell on. That's a sign of immaturity and it's self-destructive. 

Rejection doesn't mean you're a bad person; it's subjective, and it happens all the time. You'll meet lots more people. It will still suck, but don't turn it into a life-ending thing. Accept it, and respect their boundaries. 

Bystander intervention: 
1. Directly intervene and call the offender out
2. Distract the offender to stop the harmful interaction
3. Delegate and get assistance from other people to find a good solution

Majority of this advice boils down to: communicate directly and honestly, and don't do anything without an absolute up-to-date understanding of the other person's comfort level.
