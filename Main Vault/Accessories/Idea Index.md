##### Characters
**Kenny Lewis**
A hopeful child who's dad is fighting in the war. He believes he's coming back and refuses to let go of that. He hasn't killed anyone, but he's willing to fight bad guys to protect his mom. He views himself as the protector of his family, so he takes very bold risks. No matter how bad he wants to help, he's still a pretty weak and naive 9 year old. 

**Dead Man**
They know they're going to die, so they've temporarily let themselves go. They're trying to do something before their time runs out, but without any inhibitions. Similar to Roy Batty, and to Walter White. Potentially draw inspiration from the Arabian terminal lucidity story for extreme characterization. 

**Tad Strange**
In a world where everything is insane, introduce a man with absolutely nothing going on. He is surrounded by the exceptional, but he's just an accountant. He has a strikingly boring by-the-books backstory, and is a reasonable good person. He's just trying his best to accomplish his life goals. In his story, focus on his potential feelings of inadequacy, self-doubt, or boredom. His interactions are typically successful because he isn't an exaggerated character and most people aren't awed or bored by him. 

**Mystery Man**
A man who regularly interacts with people and suggests that he knows more than he lets on. He's never explained or understood, and makes people uncomfortable. Similar to the G-Man, or to the Happy Mask Salesman. The universe has rules that he flagrantly operates outside of. He has goals and intentions, and there is a method to his madness, but the protagonists aren't made to know that. 

**The Angel**
A being of such great power that they view humanity as pointless. Why care about them when you can just make more? Not actively hostile, but not actively interested. It serves its own goals, which often operate on scales indifferent to humanity. Inspired by the Mysterious Stranger by Mark Twain.

**Johnathan Carter Hayes**
An illiterate dyscalculic man with a terrible memory, no plan, and no insight. He's insecure because of his mental challenges and views everyone else as literally superior. He's a loving, encouraging, apologetic, forgiving, and brave knight in shining armor. He is commonly underestimated for his complete lack of... well most things, but he can entertain people and can be funny (intentionally or not). He likes the color green, chicken fingers, apple juice, and Weird Al Yankovic. Inspired by Charlie Kelly, Peter Parker, and Steve Harrington. 

**Wade**
A charismatic, quick witted con artist. Gets out of things by lying, stealing, tricking, and brawling. Makes reckless decisions on a whim, and appreciates quick, simple pleasures. There's very little beyond the surface, and despite being fairly selfish, he's a generally goodhearted and kind person. This is the kind of character to antagonize Cthulhu. Inspired by Nathan Drake and Frank Abagnale Jr. 

**The Conqueror**
A cunning perfectionist obsessed with military perfection. He fights honorably, but is the best at what he does. He's arrogant, greedy, selfish, and cold-blooded. All he wants is to amass more power and wealth for his kingdom. He's genuinely great at what he does, and he follows an honor-bound code, which is what makes him so terrifying; he doesn't *have* to take shortcuts or anything to come out on top cause he's already just that much smarter and more skilled. 

**William with Uber Eats**
A man who works as a delivery driver for a non-specified company. He has to deliver whatever is ordered to wherever it's requested no questions asked. So, there could be an active shooter or something, and he'd have to deliver chicken fingers. He can also be used to deliver other things. He hates his job. 
He answers questions like Dr. Arroyo from Conan O'Brien's appearance on Hot Ones. Where did you go to school? "1981." Are you even a real doctor? "I was supposed to be." It's all non-sequiturs, and it isn't clear why he's doing this awful job.


##### Worldbuilding
**Alien Space**
A place where everything uses intense, vivid, and unnatural colors. Unknowable organisms existing in massive spaces you can't understand, supported by music with long, drawn out, and deep chords, and melodies that are off and incomplete. 

**Retrofuturism**
Inspired by 80s sci-fi cyberpunk. Everything is analog, which means buttons, switches, wires, knobs, and *no* touchscreens! Oscilloscopes and computer terminals are everywhere. Everything runs on no-GUI Linux.

**Telekin**
Some people are born with 4D limbs that functionally grant telekinesis. They can be used to sense and interact with the space around the individual, over distances up to a mile. 

**Valley of Rot**
The residence of death itself. The world is bathed in darkness, ice, and rot. Metal constructs called Reapers mindlessly roam and obliterate whatever life they can find. They all operate on a central hivemind. 

**The Bazaar**
A massive complex or shops, aliens, and portals at the center of existence. 

**Underwater Cave Diving**
An isolated, dark, and creepy place where you might uncover something man was never meant to encounter. 

**Elevator Fast Travel**
A nonsensical semi-sentient (but mute) elevator that appears sporadically throughout the world that allows for fast travel. It's unclear how it works since it's not actually connected to anything and it travels impossibly fast. 

**Realistic Virtual World**
Each entity exists in RAM and is a product of the game's code. Root users can create administrators, and administrators can view people's statistics, and create / destroy objects in RAM. Sentient programs are descendants of a mother algorithm, which exists in a space left behind by the developers. 

**Xenobiology**
Alien plants that have supernatural properties. They live by consuming heat instead of sunlight, they release an intoxicating smell that causing delirium, they emit bright flashing lights to stun and trap prey, and so on. 

**Electric Magic System**
A small percentage of the population is born with access to an electric based magic system. They can create and interact with electricity and magnetic fields, including the electricity in their bodies, but they aren't immune to it. 
	Novice: enhanced reflexes, quick thinking, enhanced speed, and enhanced precision.
	Beginner: creation of magnetic fields, enhanced strength
	Intermediate: redirecting lightning
	Advanced: creation of lightning
	Master: flight, absorbing lightning
Practitioners serve an evil empire and wield long, thin needle-like swords. Majority of them don't push based beginner / intermediate. The king and his hand are pushing advanced / master though. Most people who are born with this ability are discovered quickly, and then either indoctrinated into the government or killed. Maybe the protagonist was born with a godlike potential for these abilities, and the story watches his progress from newbie to one of the most powerful characters in that universe. He goes on a crusade and adventure to free people and eventually to defeat the evil king. It's a standard plot but the action would be cool.


##### Plots
**Battleworld**
An extradimensional overseer populates the world at the dawn of each season with life, monsters, weapons, and resources. The natural ecosystem would tear itself apart in a second if not for these portals to the unknown world. There's no off-limits genres or ideas, given they follow the rules of the world and are taken seriously by the characters. It's similar to Adventure Time and Regular Show in that matter. The protagonists have a monster manual, and are assigned targets at the beginning of each session like bounty hunters. The focus is on having fun and goofing off in this silly DND world while getting loot, fighting monsters, and saving people.

**Refrain**
The players exist in a perpetual hour-long time loop. Each of them are assigned unique escape requirements that clash with the other players and demand quick thinking and adaptation. The further out from the starting point they explore, the more complex things get, and the harder the memorization becomes. 

**Cohabitation**
The players exist in a perpetual day-long time loop. Each day, they're placed into the same seemingly mundane location and day in an alternate timeline. The scenario changes wildly each time; genre parodies, aliens, cowboys, dragons, communists, zombies, etc. There are no rules, and things often escalate dramatically with little warning. Their overall goal is to learn and survive for as long as they can. Most things remain throughout each iteration, like layout and interactions. The sincerity of the story comes from the characters, and the problems in their life that persist across iterations. Inspired by Community and Majora's Mask. 

**SMS-88**
A parasite has infected a cave system on an alien planet. It can only infect people while isolated, since it emits a bunch of bright colorful lights. The party is dispatched to investigate the disappearance of the previous crew. One player is infected in the very beginning off-camera to seed the conflict, and will attempt to isolate and infect the other players. If even one infected makes it out on the ship, the infected win. Players have multiple tasks throughout the map that require unique non-sharable skillsets that encourage separation. The goal is to instill paranoia, distrust, and confusion. Have incompatible characters with incompatible motivations and incompatible attachments. Reasonable motivations + stakes lead to PvP. Players must know beforehand that infections can occur silently, and that they have to go along with it. Use The Thing (1984)'s OST. 

**The Truman Show**
The players are in an inauthentic reality, and everyone else is part of a hivemind. They need to figure out what's going on and escape. Take inspiration from [[Postmodernism, Simulacra, and Irony]]. 

**Party in the CIA**
The players are spies who are placed in foreign territory and need to complete a mission, but it goes haywire and they have to adapt to the expanding conspiracy. That's just the backdrop though, the real fun comes from taking them to absurd places and putting them in insane situations while staying completely serious. John Kiriakou type stuff. They have to sing Karaoke or lose a deal with the cartel, or they have to piss in a guy's drink to get revenge, or they have to blow up a building to prevent being made. Just the craziest stuff they can think of, but they get away with all of it because they're in the CIA. A backdrop for players to be retarded, basically. Everyone takes it completely seriously, and there's no winking, no pausing, no nothing. Anything unhinged that happens will be treated like it's normal and then moved on from. 



##### Plot Elements
**Betrayal**
Seed a betrayal from the beginning from one of the party members. Incompatible character with incompatible motivations and incompatible attachments. Give them a reasonable motivation, a ticking clock, and genuine stakes. End with a climactic competitive PVP system breakdown and get players into it.

**False Betrayal**
Create a situation in which players turn on each other whilst none of them are guilty.

**Wither Storm**
Cosmic threat that roves about the world. It's approach is the threat, giving every action along the adventure momentum. 
- https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q0yCfKzQBlw

**The Bell Ringer**
Below a small town lives a dormant eldritch deity that is beginning to wake and haunt the residents with dark visions that drive them to madness and suicide. You need to calm the affected and stop it before it's too late.

**Arctic Server**
A computer hobbyist accidentally discovers and establishes communication with an isolated server system that just came online. There, he begins communicating. The person they message quickly manipulates him into believing a constructed narrative about who he's speaking to, what they want, and why. The hobbyist is pushed into disabling locks on the system architecture. After he does this, his monitor shuts off while his computer runs a bunch of scripts. When it turns back on, the server system is gone, and his computer was formatted to erase all evidence. 

**Hunger Games**
Throw players in an arena and have them fight to the death. Tell them in advance that they're likely going to have to turn on each other. 

**The Vanishing**
A huge cargo ship with like at least 200 people crashes into the shore. There's no one on it and nothing is missing; it's as if everyone aboard just vanished while at sea. Security records turn into intense static at the time of the vanishing. 

**Firefly - Jubal Early**
Someone infiltrates your ship / camp / home while you're asleep. They have a thorough plan, complex goals, and a huge advantage. You need to play along, find your teammates, and eventually outsmart the intruder to escape whatever they have planned. 

**Adrift**
Your ship / submarine is adrift and rapidly running out of oxygen. There aren't enough escape pods. You can try to fix the ship, but eventually someone may have to be left behind. 




##### Mechanics
**Stress Management**
Have players need to recall and use complex information in tense situations. Give them a packet of in-universe information about something, and have them need to use it under a time limit to escape a monster. Horror + Stakes > Horror.

**Bad Map**
Give players plotting a heist incomplete information so that they can plan, but not enough to be fully prepared. Maintain room for unpredictability. 

**Hyper Tabletop**
Inception within tabletop; they're playing as people playing tabletop and so on. Actions made on the upper levels effect lower levels. Nearly impossible to pull off, but a neat mechanic for a session maybe. 

**Interrogation**
The party is apprehended and have to lie to escape. They may be separated, so they need to anticipate what questions may be asked and what their cover story might be. Inspired by Firefly and Community. 

**(CLASS) Engineer**
The Engineer uses an item modifier system to allow them to actually create custom items. The modifiers work like Minecraft enchantments, but offer more mechanical abilities / synergies. There are three available subclasses: 
	Formulist (potions)
	Blacksmith (weapons/tools
	Saboteur (traps/explosives). 

**(CLASS) Telekinetic**
The Telekinetic can move things with their mind. They can dish out an insane amount of damage, but they tire out fast. There are three available subclasses:
	Psion (mental influence)
	Rampart (power/quantity)
	Specter (precision/sense)

**(CLASS) Hero**
The Hero has one signature weapon that they modify and grow with throughout the whole adventure. They can recover the weapon if lost, but there are major consequences for doing so. There are three available subclasses:
	Oathbound. Makes the weapon sentient and ties the weapon's power to a vow/deal. The weapon will push you to do things that align with its interests and you can't violate the deal, but it also becomes disgustingly powerful. 
	Gearbound. Grants the weapon a signature ability that you can customize. Powerful if used properly, due to how customizable it becomes. More upkeep, but far more min-maxing. 
	Warbound. Allows you access to multiple signature weapons that each carry their own boons and can be used interchangeably. 

**(CLASS) Ruffian**
The Ruffian is your Han Solo, your Nathan Drake, your Saul Goodman. They're an incredibly persuasive, sneaky, and well rounded class built around charisma. They focus on negotiation, stealth, and trap setting. The core fantasy is scheming and cooperation. 



##### Games
**L55 Flight Simulator**
A slow, lore-based game in which you live in and manage a dead spaceship. You awake with no idea of how you got there, and are completely alone as everyone else is dead. You need to figure out what happened and how to escape as quickly as you can, as resources are running out. Uses 64 bit textures combined with 80s retrofuturism and a dash of the Portal escape sequence. 

**Station-17**
Manage a station on the moon and protect yourself from increasingly dangerous "anomalies."

**Civilization Manager**

It eventually becomes an RTS, but slightly slower and more resource intensive. You get to a point where you're deploying thousands of troops, and spending millions on deals for nuclear arms.
