DND is incredibly popular
- 14 million players
- Massive resurgence since COVID
- Dimension 20 and Critical Role

Problems with DND
- Time investment
	- Learning to play requires a lot of reading and experience
	- Running a DND campaign requires a lot of writing and math
	- Creating a character manually is very slow
- Initial cost
	- Physical books cost $180 for just the primary 3 books
	- The easier to use website costs $90 + an annual $60 fee for just the primary 3 books  
	- Additions cost far more
	- Miniatures, dice, maps, and other accessories are expensive in and of themselves
- Creative limitations
	- Though flexible, the game is built for high-fantasy epics
	- Creating your own content is frowned upon due to it being unbalanced
	- Custom content, tones, genres, or worlds are time-consuming to implement.

TTE
- Purpose
	- Easier, Cheaper, Faster, More Creative
	- Free, easy, and accessible application that allows the creation, sharing, and use of custom content
	- Comes with tools for quickly making balanced species, backgrounds, classes, items, features, and more
	- Allows for much more creativity; people can get weird and have fun with it! They can make hyper obscure things too.
	- All players need to do is select the campaign they're joining, and then they can quickly build their characters at a level of detail suited to their knowledge of the game's inner workings. 
	- Plain and simple; making stuff is fun! I want to make stuff and share it for free and play it with my friends without any headaches. It's player owned, meaning no corporate interference can make something that should be about creativity any more expensive. I want people to get weird! I want Dungeon Crawler Carl type stuff! I want a class dedicated to making poisonous and explosive foods! I want a campaign built around sign language!
- Technical
	- Runs on the TTE Ruleset (already made)
	- The tools for content creation place the content into SQL relational databases
		- Species
		- Backgrounds
		- Classes
		- Items
		- Features
		- Tags
		- Skills
		- Conditions
		- NPCs / Monsters
		- etc.
	- These are hosted and shared from a set of powerful servers. 
		- Faster download times
		- Near impossible to shut down
		- Free hosting
		- Everything goes through initial checks against offensive or illegal content
		- Rating system to allow people to see new / high quality content
		- Reporting feature for offensive or copyrighted content
			- Reports are sent to a moderation team
			- Things that are flagged the most are prioritized
			- Mods decide if it violates the rules
			- If it does, it's removed from the search so that only the creator can view it
	- The client software can then process the database to create characters 
- Art style
	- Creative focus: arts and crafts / Little Big Planet
	- Disarming and inviting. 
- Financial
	- I'm making this predominately because I believe in the idea, not for profit. With that said, I need to support a small team.
	- I won't charge a subscription for access to the content. This goes against the reasons for creating it in the first place, I won't run ads because it's once again unnecessary. If I am charging for the product, it'll be an up-front, reasonable charge. Probably approximately 20-40 dollars for the software.
