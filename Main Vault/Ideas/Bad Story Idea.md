A story that's about why we do what's right

The villain is a practical man. He does bad things for his own gain because he thinks it's all really relative.
The hero is a good man. He does what is right and regularly suffers for it. He's condemned to an unending struggle.

The hero tries to stop the villain, and the villain repeatedly makes his life hell. It gets worse and worse, until the hero finally overcomes evil. The villain questions why he'd do all of this if it's just making his life terrible. 

"I'm not trying to win. I'm not doing this because I want to beat someone. It's not because it's fun. God knows it's not because it's easy. I do what I do because it's right, because it's decent, and above all it's kind. If I run away today, good people will die. If I stand and fight, some of them might live. Maybe there's no point, but it's the best I can do."

Kind of like a Spider-Man / Doctor Who type lifestyle. His life just sucks because of his heroism.
